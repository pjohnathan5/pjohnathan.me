Einstein's Secret Weapon: Sleep

Albert Einstein was not only one of the greatest minds in human history but also one of its most dedicated sleepers, famously prioritizing ten hours of rest each night as a non-negotiable part of his routine. He believed deeply that sleep was not a luxury but a necessity, treating it with the same seriousness he gave to his scientific work and refusing to let social obligations or deadlines cut into his rest. Einstein often credited his most vivid and breakthrough ideas to the hypnagogic state between wakefulness and sleep, a liminal zone where his imagination ran free from the constraints of conventional thinking. He was also known to supplement his long nights with short naps throughout the day, keeping a notepad nearby to capture any ideas that surfaced in those drowsy in-between moments. For Einstein, sleep was not downtime but prime time, a biological laboratory where the subconscious mind quietly solved the problems that the waking mind could not.
